Economic Fairness

Economic Fairness for people of color consists of the capacity of all people to earn a decent and justifiable living in justifiable work environments. It is the discourse on fair trade, entrepreneurship, developing capital, running successful innovative businesses, and attaining leadership positions in numerous industries and sectors.

The Black Institute aims to engage communities of color and other stakeholders in the continuous discussion on developing solutions in acquiring capital.

Fast Food Campaign

Ongoing

TBI helped launch one the nation’s most successful economic justice campaigns – the fast food campaign. In 2012, when the campaign first began in New York City, TBI partnered with New York Communities for Change to hold meetings in boroughs across NYC with majority black clergy and fast food workers. Workers shared their stories of low pay and little respect at the workplace and the stories mirrored the issues faced by thousands in the clergy’s congregations- most of whom lived below the poverty line. After hearing these stories, the clergy quickly identified poverty wages in the fast food industry as an issue they wanted to work on.
